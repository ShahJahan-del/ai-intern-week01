# Insights - Titanic

1. **Mostly young passengers :** The majority of the population on board was very young, with a great representation of the 25-28 age group.
2. **Big presence of babies and toddlers :** After the 25-28 age group peak, there is another smaller but noticeable one for the 0-4 age group, meaning that there were lots of babies and toddlers on board too.
3. **Mass transportation :** The scatter plot shows that there is a high concentration of fares under 100$, most of them not even being higher than 50$. This shows the Titanic was mostly a mass transportation ship and most of the passengers were of modest classes.
4. **Presence of 2 outliers :** There two points in the scatter plot that show how far the prices can go. In this context, it is not unbelievable that the richest passengers bought expensive tickets for better suites.
5. **Fare and age not related :** The scatter plot is fairly horizontal in its distribution of points. The age doesn't really seem related to the fare.
